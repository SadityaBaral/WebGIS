Traverse

In surveying, a traverse is a method used to establish a series of connected points by measuring the distances and angles between them. Theodolites or Total Stations are used to measure angles and distances. There are two types: an open traverse and a closed traverse. This technique is especially useful in construction, road planning, and land development, where exact boundaries and layouts are essential.Traverse surveys are popular because they allow for accurate mapping in areas where other methods, like GPS, might not work well, such as dense forests, urban environments, or rugged terrain.

Calculation

It involves measuring distances and angles between stations and then using trigonometry to calculate coordinates. First, the angles are adjusted and bearings are calculated for each leg of the traverse.Then Changing in eastings and northings are then computed. The final station's coordinates are compared to the starting point, and any closing errors are adjusted using methods like the Bowditch Rule.
